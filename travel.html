Travel Tips

Kotoka International Airport (ACC) in Accra is the main entry point, with direct flights from Europe, the Middle East, and other African countries. Ensure your passport is valid for at least six months and check visa requirements before travel.
Airport Transfers: Taxis and ride-hailing services like Uber and Bolt are available at the airport. Arrange airport pickup with your hotel if possible for convenience and safety.
Domestic Travel: Domestic flights connect Accra to Kumasi, Tamale, and Takoradi. Intercity buses and minibuses (trotros) are affordable options for overland travel.
The official currency is the Ghanaian Cedi (GHS). Credit cards are accepted in major hotels and restaurants, but cash is recommended for local markets and smaller towns. ATMs are widely available in cities.
Currency Exchange: Exchange money at banks or licensed forex bureaus for the best rates. Avoid street money changers.
Tipping: Tipping is appreciated but not mandatory; 5-10% is customary in restaurants.
Ghana has a tropical climate with a wet season (April–October) and a dry season (November–March). Pack lightweight clothing, sunscreen, and stay hydrated, especially during the hot months.
Rainy Season: Roads may become muddy or flooded in rural areas. Carry a light raincoat or umbrella.
Harmattan: From December to February, dry and dusty winds may affect air quality; consider a mask if you have respiratory issues.
Ghana offers a range of accommodations, from luxury hotels in Accra to guesthouses and eco-lodges in rural areas. Book in advance during peak travel seasons and major festivals.
Booking Tips: Check reviews and amenities before booking. Some rural areas may have limited options, so plan accordingly.
Safety: Use hotel safes for valuables and lock your doors at night.
Local SIM cards are available from providers like MTN, Vodafone, and AirtelTigo. Internet access is reliable in urban areas, with 4G coverage in most cities. Wi-Fi is available in hotels and cafes.
SIM Registration: You will need your passport to register a SIM card.
Emergency Numbers: Police: 191, Ambulance: 193, Fire: 192.
Enjoy vibrant nightlife, live music, and cultural festivals throughout the year. Accra and Kumasi offer cinemas, clubs, and art galleries. Local cuisine and street food are a must-try.
Festivals: Don’t miss events like Homowo, Panafest, and Chale Wote Street Art Festival.
Shopping: Visit local markets for crafts, fabrics, and souvenirs. Bargaining is common.
Major cities have well-equipped hospitals and clinics. It is recommended to have travel insurance and ensure routine vaccinations are up to date. Malaria prophylaxis is advised; carry basic medications and consult your doctor before travel.
Health Precautions: Drink bottled or filtered water. Avoid raw foods and unpeeled fruits.
Pharmacies: Pharmacies are common in cities and towns, but bring any prescription medications you need.
Ghana is generally safe, but petty crime like pickpocketing can occur, especially in crowded areas.
Travel Tips: Keep your belongings secure, avoid walking alone at night, and be cautious when using ATMs.
Local Laws: Respect local customs and laws. Ask permission before photographing people or sensitive sites.
Public transport includes buses, trotros (minibuses), and taxis. Ride-hailing apps are available in major cities.
Car Hire: Car rentals are available, but driving can be challenging due to road conditions and traffic.
Road Safety: Wear seatbelts and avoid night travel outside major cities.
